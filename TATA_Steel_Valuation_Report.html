Appendix A: WACC Build-Up Detail

Risk-Free Rate (Rf) = 6.70% [India 10Y G-Sec, Mar 2026] Equity Risk Premium (ERP) = 7.00% [Damodaran, India ERP 2025] Beta (β) = 1.20 [5Y monthly; adjusted toward 1] Cost of Equity (Ke) = Rf + β × ERP = 6.70 + 1.20 × 7.00 = 6.70 + 8.40 = 15.10% Pre-Tax Cost of Debt (Kd) = 7.50% [Wtd. avg INR + foreign debt] Corporate Tax Rate (t) = 25.17% Post-Tax Cost of Debt = 7.50% × (1 − 0.2517) = 7.50% × 0.7483 = 5.61% Capital Structure (Book-Value Weights used in DCF): Debt Weight (D/V) = 51.0% Equity Weight (E/V) = 49.0% WACC = (E/V × Ke) + (D/V × Kd × (1−t)) = (0.49 × 15.10%) + (0.51 × 5.61%) = 7.40% + 2.86% = 10.26%

Appendix C: Glossary for MBA Students

TermDefinition
FCFFFree Cash Flow to the Firm — cash available to all capital providers (debt + equity) after operating expenses and capex
WACCWeighted Average Cost of Capital — blended cost of equity and after-tax debt, weighted by capital structure
EV/EBITDAEnterprise Value ÷ Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortization — capital-structure-neutral valuation multiple
Terminal ValuePresent value of all cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period; computed via Gordon Growth Model
Beta (β)Measure of a stock's sensitivity to market movements; β > 1 means more volatile than the market
CAPMCapital Asset Pricing Model: Ke = Rf + β × (Rm − Rf); links expected return to systematic risk
Mid-Cycle EBITDANormalized EBITDA averaged through a full commodity cycle; avoids distortion from peak/trough earnings
Football Field ChartVisual summary of valuation ranges across multiple methodologies; shows overlap and spread
DuPont AnalysisDecomposition of ROE into Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier

Appendix E: Common MBA Interview / Viva Questions

#QuestionQuick Answer
1 Why is terminal value so large? Explicit forecast only 5 years; most value accrues beyond the forecast period in a DCF
2 Why not use P/E for steel? Cyclical earnings make P/E unreliable; EV/EBITDA is capital-structure-neutral and more stable
3 Why is DDM value so low? Low payout ratio; steel firms reinvest heavily in capex and debt repayment
4 Biggest risk to valuation? WACC assumption — a 1% change shifts intrinsic value by ₹20–40 per share
5 How to cross-check DCF? Relative valuation (comps), sum-of-parts, and reverse DCF (implied growth rate check)
6 Why use FCFF not FCFE? FCFF is pre-debt; better for highly leveraged companies where capital structure may change
7 What is Tata Steel's moat? Lowest-cost integrated producer with 100% captive iron ore in India
