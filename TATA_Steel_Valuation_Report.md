## 6. Valuation Methodologies

### A. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) — FCFF Model

#### Step 1: WACC Calculation

| Component | Value | Source / Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| Risk-Free Rate (Rf) | 6.70% | India 10Y G-Sec (Mar 2026) |
| Equity Risk Premium (ERP) | 7.00% | India ERP (Incwert/Damodaran 2025+) |
| Beta (β) | 1.20 | 5Y adjusted beta |
| Cost of Equity (Ke) | 15.10% | Rf + β × ERP = 6.70 + 1.20 × 7.00 |
| Pre-Tax Cost of Debt (Kd) | 7.50% | Weighted avg. of INR & foreign borrowings |
| Tax Rate (t) | 25.17% | Indian statutory corporate tax |
| Post-Tax Cost of Debt | 5.61% | 7.50% × (1 − 0.2517) |
| Debt Weight (D/V) | 51.0% | |
| Equity Weight (E/V) | 49.0% | |
| **WACC** | **10.26%** | **(0.49 × 15.10) + (0.51 × 5.61)** |

#### Step 2: FCFF Projections

```
FCFF = EBIT × (1 − Tax Rate) + Depreciation − Capex − ΔWorking Capital
```

| Year | EBIT | EBIT×(1−t) | + Dep. | − Capex | − ΔWC | FCFF |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY2026E | 19,399 | 14,517 | 11,000 | 16,000 | 3,508 | 6,009 |
| FY2027E | 23,884 | 17,872 | 11,800 | 17,000 | 3,823 | 8,849 |
| FY2028E | 29,556 | 22,118 | 12,500 | 15,000 | 2,804 | 16,814 |
| FY2029E | 34,135 | 25,546 | 12,800 | 13,000 | 3,028 | 22,318 |
| FY2030E | 37,221 | 27,854 | 13,000 | 12,000 | 3,240 | 25,614 |

#### Step 3: Terminal Value

```
TV = FCFF₂₀₃₀ × (1 + g) / (WACC − g)
   = 25,614 × 1.045 / 0.0575
   = ₹4,65,520 Crore
```

#### Step 4: Present Value Calculation

| FY2030E | 25,614 | 0.6139 | 15,724 |

### Appendix A: WACC Build-Up Detail

```
Risk-Free Rate (Rf)          =  6.70%   [India 10Y G-Sec, Mar 2026]
Equity Risk Premium (ERP)    =  7.00%   [Damodaran, India ERP 2025]
Beta (β)                     =  1.20    [5Y monthly; adjusted toward 1]
Cost of Equity (Ke)          = Rf + β × ERP
                             = 6.70 + 1.20 × 7.00
                             = 6.70 + 8.40
                             = 15.10%

Pre-Tax Cost of Debt (Kd)    =  7.50%   [Wtd. avg INR + foreign debt]
Corporate Tax Rate (t)       = 25.17%
Post-Tax Cost of Debt        = 7.50% × (1 − 0.2517)
                             = 7.50% × 0.7483
                             = 5.61%

Capital Structure:
  Equity Value (E)           = ₹2,47,517 Cr (12,480 Mn × ₹198.46)
  Total Debt (D)             = ₹94,801 Cr
  Total Capital (V)          = E + D = ₹3,42,318 Cr
  E/V                        = 72.3%  ← market weights
  D/V                        = 27.7%

  [Note: For WACC, book-value weights used in DCF model above:
   D/V = 51%, E/V = 49% — conservative leveraged capital structure]

WACC = (E/V × Ke) + (D/V × Kd × (1−t))
     = (0.49 × 15.10%) + (0.51 × 5.61%)
     = 7.40% + 2.86%
     = 10.26%
```

### Appendix C: Glossary for MBA Students

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **FCFF** | Free Cash Flow to the Firm — cash available to all capital providers (debt + equity) after operating expenses and capex |
| **WACC** | Weighted Average Cost of Capital — blended cost of equity and after-tax debt, weighted by capital structure |
| **EV/EBITDA** | Enterprise Value ÷ Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortization — capital-structure-neutral valuation multiple |
| **Terminal Value** | Present value of all cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period; computed via Gordon Growth Model |
| **Beta (β)** | Measure of a stock's sensitivity to market movements; β > 1 means more volatile than the market |
| **CAPM** | Capital Asset Pricing Model: Ke = Rf + β × (Rm − Rf); links expected return to systematic risk |
| **Mid-Cycle EBITDA** | Normalized EBITDA averaged through a full commodity cycle; avoids distortion from peak/trough earnings |
| **Football Field Chart** | Visual summary of valuation ranges across multiple methodologies; shows overlap and spread |
| **DuPont Analysis** | Decomposition of ROE into Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier |
